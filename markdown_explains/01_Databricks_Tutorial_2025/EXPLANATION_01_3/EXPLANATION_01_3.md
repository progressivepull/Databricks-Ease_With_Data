# EXPLANATION 01 3

**Querying and Analysis in a Data Warehouse**

A **data warehouse** is designed specifically for **querying, reporting, and analyzing large amounts of historical data** to support business decision-making.

**What is Querying?**

Querying means retrieving specific information from the data warehouse using tools such as SQL.

**Example:**

<span class="mark">SELECT SUM(sales)\
FROM Sales\
WHERE year = 2025;</span>

This query calculates total sales for 2025.

**What is Analysis?**

Analysis involves examining data to identify trends, patterns, and insights.

**Examples:**

- Analyzing monthly sales trends.

- Comparing product performance across regions.

- Identifying top customers.

- Forecasting future demand.

**Key Characteristics**

- **Optimized for read operations** rather than frequent updates.

- Stores **historical and integrated data** from multiple sources.

- Supports **Business Intelligence (BI)**, dashboards, and reporting tools.

- Enables **fast execution of complex queries**.

**Example Scenario**

A retail company uses a data warehouse to analyze:

- Total sales by year.

- Best-selling products.

- Customer purchasing trends.

- Regional performance comparisons.

**Simple Definition**

**Querying and analysis in a data warehouse refer to retrieving, examining, and interpreting large volumes of historical data to generate business insights and support decision-making.**
