# EXPLANATION 02 1

**Business Intelligence (BI)**

Business Intelligence (BI) is the process of collecting, analyzing, visualizing, and reporting data to help organizations make better business decisions.

In simple terms:

BI turns raw data into useful insights.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Simple Real-World Example**

Imagine a retail company.

Every day it generates data such as:

- sales transactions

- customer purchases

- inventory updates

- employee performance

- shipping information

Raw data alone is hard to understand.

BI tools help answer questions like:

- Which products sell the most?

- Which store performs best?

- Are sales increasing or decreasing?

- Which region generates the highest revenue?

- What products are low in stock?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**What BI Typically Includes**

**1. Reporting**

Static or scheduled reports showing business metrics.

Example:

Monthly Revenue Report\
Quarterly Profit Summary\
Employee Performance Report

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**2. Dashboards**

Interactive visual displays of KPIs and trends.

Examples:

- sales dashboards

- finance dashboards

- customer analytics dashboards

Common visuals:

- bar charts

- pie charts

- line graphs

- KPI cards

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**3. Data Analysis**

BI tools allow users to:

- filter data

- drill down into details

- compare trends

- analyze performance

Example:

Revenue by Region\
Revenue by Product\
Revenue by Month

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**4. Data Visualization**

BI converts complex datasets into easy-to-understand visuals.

Instead of reading thousands of rows:

Date \| Product \| Revenue

You see:

- charts

- graphs

- heatmaps

- trend lines

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**How BI Works**

Typical BI flow:

Data Sources\
↓\
Data Warehouse / Lakehouse\
↓\
BI Tool\
↓\
Dashboards & Reports\
↓\
Business Decisions

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Common Data Sources**

BI tools can connect to:

- SQL databases

- Data warehouses

- Data lakehouses

- Cloud storage

- Excel files

- APIs

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**BI vs Data Science**

| **Business Intelligence**           | **Data Science**                      |
|-------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| Focuses on reporting and dashboards | Focuses on prediction and ML          |
| Explains what happened              | Predicts what may happen              |
| Uses charts and KPIs                | Uses algorithms and models            |
| Mostly descriptive analytics        | Predictive and prescriptive analytics |

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**BI in Databricks**

In Databricks, BI capabilities include:

- Databricks SQL

- dashboards

- queries

- KPI visualization

- integration with Power BI/Tableau

Because Databricks uses a Lakehouse architecture:

- analysts

- engineers

- data scientists

can all use the same centralized data.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Example BI Questions**

A company may use BI to answer:

- What were total sales last quarter?

- Which customers generate the most profit?

- Which products are underperforming?

- What is the customer churn rate?

- Are operational costs increasing?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Key Benefits of BI**

| **Benefit**         | **Description**         |
|---------------------|-------------------------|
| Better decisions    | Data-driven insights    |
| Faster reporting    | Automated dashboards    |
| Improved visibility | Real-time monitoring    |
| Trend analysis      | Understand patterns     |
| KPI tracking        | Monitor business health |

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**One-Sentence Summary**

**Business Intelligence (BI)** is the use of data analysis, reporting, and visualization tools to help organizations make informed business decisions.
